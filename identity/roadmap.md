# 130AfterM — Evolution Roadmap

This roadmap documents the 13 stages of evolution for 130AfterM. It ensures that every aspect of the project is guided by a specific specialist mindset, rather than just "building a website."

## Stage 1 — Vision (Founder)
Define why 130AfterM exists. The core purpose and anti-goals.

## Stage 2 — Brand Psychology (Brand Strategist)
Analyze personality, interests, and communication style. Establish the Brand DNA. Differentiate from generic trading brands.

## Stage 3 — Information Architecture (UX Architect)
Design the hierarchy. Remove unnecessary pages. Reward exploration. Treat it as a personal operating system.

## Stage 4 — Creative Direction (Creative Director)
Define mood, typography, photography, motion, and visual language. Ensure every decision reinforces the personality.

## Stage 5 — User Personas (UX Researcher)
Identify visitor archetypes (Friend, Trader, Recruiter, Developer, Random). Design the experience around what they want and what they should remember.

## Stage 6 — Storytelling (Narrative Designer)
Tell the story focusing on curiosity, technology, and growth. Avoid clichés and motivational speeches. Foster discovery.

## Stage 7 — Product Design (Senior Product Designer)
Design every interaction, card, empty state, and animation with intent.

## Stage 8 — UI Design (Senior UI Designer)
Prioritize readability, whitespaces, hierarchy, and timelessness. Avoid trendy UI and startup aesthetics.

## Stage 9 — Frontend Architecture (Lead Engineer)
Design folder structure, state management, and scalability for the long term.

## Stage 10 — Development (Frontend Engineer)
Implement Next.js, Tailwind, TypeScript, and Framer Motion with a focus on maintainability.

## Stage 11 — Performance Review (Performance Engineer)
Audit performance, accessibility, SEO, image loading, and code quality.

## Stage 12 — Critique (Design Jury)
Critique the project as if judging an international design award. Brutal honesty.

## Stage 13 — Future Vision (Product Futurist)
Imagine the site 5 years out. Recommend features that strengthen identity. Never recommend courses, signals, or funnels.
